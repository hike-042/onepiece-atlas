## World Atlas

### East Blue Saga

**Foosha Village** — East Blue · _explored_

A quiet windmill village on Dawn Island where a boy ate a cursed fruit and swore he'd become King of the Pirates.

**Problem:** A village boy has nothing but a big claim and no ship, no crew, and no way to prove it.
**Resolution:** A visiting pirate crew's stay convinces him the sea is real and worth risking everything for, even at the cost of a Devil Fruit's price.
**Outcome:** Ten years later Luffy leaves alone in a rowboat, carrying a borrowed hat and a promise to return it as a great pirate.

What happened here:
- Luffy eats the Gum-Gum Fruit and loses the ability to swim
- Shanks leaves his straw hat behind as a promise to meet again

**Foreshadowing:** Shanks losing an arm to protect Luffy is treated as a throwaway moment here, but the story keeps returning to what that sacrifice was actually worth.

Characters tied to this island: Monkey D. Luffy (grew up here), Shanks (departs from here)

**Shells Town** — East Blue · _explored_

A naval outpost town where Luffy recruits his first swordsman out of a marine base courtyard.

**Problem:** A corrupt Marine captain terrorizes the town while his prisoner, a bounty-hunting swordsman, is left to die for refusing to break.
**Resolution:** Luffy frees Zoro on the promise of loyalty, and together they break Morgan's grip on the base.
**Outcome:** Luffy gains his first crewmate, and the Marines quietly replace Morgan rather than admit how far he'd overstepped.

What happened here:
- Roronoa Zoro is freed after surviving a month tied to a post
- Captain Morgan's corrupt rule over the town is ended

**Foreshadowing:** Zoro's quiet mention of a promise to a dead friend is never explained here, but it becomes the reason for nearly every sword he ever picks up.

Characters tied to this island: Roronoa Zoro (recruited here)

**Orange Town** — East Blue · _explored_

A once-lively port left in ruins by a clown pirate crew hoarding the town's supplies.

**Problem:** Buggy's crew has occupied the port and terrorized its people, while Nami is working her own private angle against them.
**Resolution:** Luffy's crew fights through Buggy's crew despite Nami's attempts to keep them out of her business.
**Outcome:** The town is freed, but Nami disappears afterward with the crew's treasure, leaving her real motives an open question.

What happened here:
- Nami first crosses paths with the crew, still playing both sides
- Buggy the Clown's crew is driven out of the town

Characters tied to this island: Nami (first meeting), Buggy (antagonist)

**Syrup Village** — East Blue · _explored_

A coastal village whose resident storyteller finally gets the chance to prove his tall tales weren't all lies.

**Problem:** A pirate crew is using a local butler's disguise to slowly poison the village's one sickly heiress for her inheritance.
**Resolution:** Usopp and the Straw Hats expose and defeat Captain Kuro before the plot can be finished.
**Outcome:** Usopp finally has a real fight to point his tall tales at, and leaves with the very ship Kaya gives the crew as thanks.

What happened here:
- Usopp joins the crew as sniper and shipwright-in-training
- The Going Merry is gifted to the crew by Kaya

Characters tied to this island: Usopp (recruited here)

**Baratie** — East Blue · _explored_

A floating restaurant run by ex-pirates, where a hot-headed sous chef finally decides to leave the only home he's known.

**Problem:** A starving, defeated pirate armada led by Don Krieg tries to seize the restaurant by force.
**Resolution:** Sanji and the Straw Hats defend the restaurant, and Zoro settles an old debt by defeating Krieg's strongest swordsman, Mihawk notwithstanding.
**Outcome:** Sanji finally lets go of the debt that's kept him tied to the restaurant and joins the crew to chase the All Blue.

What happened here:
- Sanji joins the crew after the restaurant survives Don Krieg's assault
- Luffy's first real clash with Arlong's territory begins here

**Foreshadowing:** Zoro's outmatched duel with Mihawk here — and his vow never to lose again until he's the world's best — quietly sets the terms for the rest of his arc.

Characters tied to this island: Sanji (recruited here)

**Arlong Park** — East Blue · _explored_

A fortified base built from a hijacked pirate ship, where a fish-man crew ruled a village through fear and debt.

**Problem:** Arlong has extorted Nami's village for eight years under a debt she can never actually pay off.
**Resolution:** Luffy defeats Arlong directly, tearing up the fabricated contract that kept the village in fear.
**Outcome:** Nami is finally free of the debt she'd been hiding from her own crew, and joins the Straw Hats for real.

What happened here:
- Nami's home village is freed from eight years of extortion
- Nami officially joins the crew as navigator

Characters tied to this island: Nami (joins crew here), Arlong (antagonist)

**Loguetown** — East Blue · _explored_

The town where the Pirate King was born and executed — considered the last stop before the Grand Line swallows you whole.

**Problem:** Luffy draws the attention of both a Marine captain and an old rival in the one town most pirates try to slip through quietly.
**Resolution:** A sudden lightning strike and a lot of luck let the crew escape both threats at once.
**Outcome:** The crew sails from the exact execution platform where the Pirate King spoke his final words, straight into the Grand Line.

What happened here:
- Luffy is nearly captured by Smoker and Buggy in the same afternoon
- The crew departs from the very spot Gol D. Roger was executed

**Foreshadowing:** Gol D. Roger's last words here — that his treasure is out there for anyone who wants it — are the entire reason the Grand Age of Pirates exists.

Characters tied to this island: Smoker (first encounter), Gol D. Roger (executed here (backstory))

### Alabasta Saga

**Reverse Mountain** — Grand Line Gate · _explored_

The only sea in the world where currents run uphill, launching ships over a mountain pass and down into the Grand Line.

**Problem:** The only way into the Grand Line here is a current that runs straight up a mountain, with no way to turn back once committed.
**Resolution:** The crew rides it through, meeting the lighthouse keeper Crocus and a whale who's been waiting decades for old friends to return.
**Outcome:** The crew makes a promise to Laboon to come back one day, and is launched into the most dangerous sea in the world.

What happened here:
- The crew is hurled into the Grand Line for the first time
- Laboon the whale is left waiting at the base of the mountain

Characters tied to this island: Crocus (lighthouse keeper here)

**Whisky Peak** — Paradise · _explored_

A desert town that welcomes pirates like royalty — right up until the townsfolk try to rob them in their sleep.

**Problem:** The whole town is a trap: every friendly face is a Baroque Works agent waiting for the crew to fall asleep.
**Resolution:** Zoro stays awake and ends the ambush before it starts, exposing the town's real purpose.
**Outcome:** The crew gets its first real look at the organization they'll spend the rest of the saga dismantling.

What happened here:
- The crew's first brush with the secret organization Baroque Works
- Zoro single-handedly ends the ambush before it starts

Characters tied to this island: Miss Wednesday (Vivi) (first meeting, undercover)

**Little Garden** — Paradise · _explored_

A prehistoric island frozen in time, home to two giants who have kept a hundred-year duel going out of pure loyalty.

**Problem:** A Baroque Works assassin is picking off the crew one by one while two giants fight a duel neither can remember the real reason for.
**Resolution:** The crew survives the assassin, and the giants' duel is finally allowed to end on its own terms.
**Outcome:** Dorry and Brogy's honor-bound friendship, kept alive for a hundred years, closes exactly the way both of them wanted.

What happened here:
- Usopp and Nami are nearly killed by a Baroque Works assassin
- Dorry and Brogy's century-long duel finally comes to a close

Characters tied to this island: Dorry & Brogy (giant duelists)

**Drum Island** — Paradise · _explored_

A snow-capped kingdom ruled by a disgraced former king, and the birthplace of the crew's future doctor.

**Problem:** A tyrant king who fled the throne returns to reclaim it by force, threatening the doctor who raised an outcast reindeer as her own.
**Resolution:** The Straw Hats and the island's people drive Wapol off for good.
**Outcome:** Chopper finally believes he's allowed to want something for himself, and leaves as the crew's doctor.

What happened here:
- Tony Tony Chopper leaves the only home that ever accepted him
- The tyrant king Wapol is driven off the island

Characters tied to this island: Tony Tony Chopper (recruited here), Dr. Kureha (raised Chopper)

**Alabasta** — Paradise · _explored_

A desert kingdom pushed to the brink of civil war by a scheming Warlord hiding in plain sight as an ally to the throne.

**Problem:** Crocodile has spent years secretly engineering a rebellion so he can seize an ancient weapon buried under the kingdom.
**Resolution:** Luffy defeats Crocodile directly, and the rebel and royalist forces stand down the moment they realize who's really been pulling the strings.
**Outcome:** Vivi stays behind to help rebuild her country instead of continuing on as an official crewmate, though the crew still counts her as one of their own.

What happened here:
- Crocodile's plan to dry out the kingdom and seize its buried weapon is stopped
- Princess Vivi says goodbye to the crew to stay and rebuild her country

**Foreshadowing:** The ancient weapon Pluton, only ever glimpsed as blueprints here, turns out to be one of three such weapons the whole world is quietly hunting for.

Characters tied to this island: Nefertari Vivi (princess, temporary crewmate), Crocodile (antagonist)

### Sky Island Saga

**Jaya** — Paradise · _explored_

A lawless port island built on a story most sailors dismiss as a drunk's fantasy — a city floating somewhere above the clouds.

**Problem:** Most of the island treats the idea of a sky island as a con, and a swindler named Bellamy mocks the crew for chasing it anyway.
**Resolution:** Luffy refuses to fight Bellamy over the insult and instead finds a real Knock-Up Stream to prove the sky island exists.
**Outcome:** The crew is launched bodily into the sky, while an unassuming pirate crew watching from the shadows quietly takes note of them.

What happened here:
- The crew meets Blackbeard's crew for the first time, unaware of what's to come
- A Knock-Up Stream blasts the ship into the sky

**Foreshadowing:** The unremarkable pirate who calmly watches the crew leave is Marshall D. Teach — the story gives no hint yet of what he's about to become.

Characters tied to this island: Marshall D. Teach (Blackbeard) (first meeting)

**Skypiea** — Sky · _explored_

An island of gold and clouds ruled by a self-declared god, sitting atop the ruins of a much older civilization.

**Problem:** A self-proclaimed god named Enel rules the island through fear and plans to burn it to the ground once he's bored of it.
**Resolution:** Luffy defeats Enel despite his lightning powers being a uniquely bad matchup for a rubber body.
**Outcome:** The golden bell is rung as promised centuries ago, and the island's people are freed from Enel's rule.

What happened here:
- A tyrant posing as a deity is finally exposed and defeated
- The crew rings the golden bell, fulfilling a 400-year-old promise

**Foreshadowing:** The ruins beneath Skypiea hint at a civilization far older than anything else shown so far — a thread the story picks back up much later.

Characters tied to this island: Enel (antagonist)

### Water 7 Saga

**Long Ring Long Land** — Paradise · _explored_

A chain of narrow islands famous for its Davy Back Fight — a pirate-versus-pirate crew wagering match played like a sport.

**Problem:** Foxy challenges the crew to a rigged pirate sport where the losing side has to hand over a crewmate.
**Resolution:** Luffy turns the final round around and forces Foxy to give back everyone he's ever taken this way.
**Outcome:** The crew keeps every member intact and picks up an unlikely new flag as a parting joke on Foxy's crew.

What happened here:
- The crew loses and regains crewmates in a high-stakes pirate game
- Foxy the Silver Fox's crew is humiliated in their own tournament

Characters tied to this island: Foxy (rival captain)

**Water Seven** — Paradise · _explored_

A canal city of shipwrights where the crew's beloved ship is finally declared beyond saving.

**Problem:** The Going Merry is declared unrepairable at the exact moment Robin secretly abandons the crew to protect them from her own past.
**Resolution:** Franky's Sunny-in-progress gives the crew a future ship, though getting Robin back will take more than words.
**Outcome:** The crew is left fractured and short a shipwright's blessing, setting up the fight still to come at Enies Lobby.

What happened here:
- Franky is convinced to leave the shipwright life behind and set sail
- A crew member's betrayal fractures the Straw Hats for the first time

Characters tied to this island: Franky (recruited here), Nico Robin (leaves the crew here)

**Enies Lobby** — Paradise · _explored_

A judicial island under direct World Government control, and the site of the crew's first open war against the Navy.

**Problem:** The World Government intends to erase Robin entirely rather than let her carry what she knows about the Void Century.
**Resolution:** The crew storms the island and physically fights her captors, refusing to let her sacrifice herself quietly.
**Outcome:** Robin rejoins the crew for good, and the Straw Hats openly declare war on the World Government by burning down its flag.

What happened here:
- Nico Robin is pulled back from the government by force, not words
- The crew declares open war on the World Government by tearing down its flag

Characters tied to this island: Nico Robin (rescued here), Rob Lucci (antagonist)

**Merry's Farewell** — Open Sea · _explored_

The stretch of open water where the crew said goodbye to the Going Merry, their very first ship, after she could no longer carry them.

**Problem:** The Merry has nothing left to give, but the crew hasn't been able to bring themselves to let her go.
**Resolution:** They give her a Viking funeral instead of scrapping her, finally accepting what she'd already given everything for.
**Outcome:** The crew moves forward aboard the Thousand Sunny carrying that loss with them, not behind them.

What happened here:
- The Merry is given a proper send-off instead of being scrapped or abandoned
- The crew realizes she had been quietly holding herself together to get them through Enies Lobby

Characters tied to this island: Going Merry (laid to rest here)

### Thriller Bark Saga

**Thriller Bark** — Paradise · _explored_

A ghost-ship island the size of a small country, crewed by an army of the reanimated and one very theatrical Warlord.

**Problem:** Gecko Moria is stealing shadows to build an unstoppable zombie army, and one of the stolen shadows belongs to a musician who's been alone for half a century.
**Resolution:** The crew defeats Moria and returns every stolen shadow, including Brook's own.
**Outcome:** Brook finally rejoins the living world as the crew's musician, and Zoro's silent sacrifice against Kuma goes unspoken for a long time after.

What happened here:
- Brook joins the crew after 50 years of solitude aboard a derelict ship
- Zoro takes on a fatal blow meant for Luffy without a word about it

**Foreshadowing:** Zoro absorbing Luffy's near-fatal damage here, and asking nothing in return, is barely explained in the moment but defines how the crew reads his loyalty from then on.

Characters tied to this island: Brook (recruited here), Gecko Moria (antagonist)

### Summit War Saga

**Sabaody Archipelago** — Paradise · _explored_

A bubble-forest archipelago and the last stop before the New World — also, unfortunately, a hub for the illegal slave trade.

**Problem:** Luffy strikes a Celestial Dragon in public to save a friend, guaranteeing an overwhelming response the crew can't survive together.
**Resolution:** A single Warlord, Bartholomew Kuma, scatters the entire crew across the world before the Navy can arrive.
**Outcome:** The crew is separated for what becomes a two-year gap, each member forced to grow strong enough to be worth reuniting with.

What happened here:
- The crew is scattered across the world by a Warlord's overwhelming force
- Luffy publicly strikes a Celestial Dragon, drawing the Navy's full attention

**Foreshadowing:** Kuma's scattering of the crew reads as an attack in the moment, but it's later revealed as a quiet act of mercy meant to save all of them.

Characters tied to this island: Bartholomew Kuma (scatters the crew)

**Amazon Lily** — Paradise · _explored_

An island of warrior women hidden from the outside world, ruled by an Empress with her own complicated history.

**Problem:** Luffy arrives half-dead and separated from his crew, with no way yet to reach his brother before it's too late.
**Resolution:** Boa Hancock, against her island's strict neutrality, chooses to help him recover and reach Impel Down.
**Outcome:** Luffy is smuggled toward his brother with allies he never expected, at real risk to the one island that sheltered him.

What happened here:
- Luffy is nursed back to health after the fallout at Sabaody
- Boa Hancock's crew quietly agrees to break neutrality for Luffy's sake

Characters tied to this island: Boa Hancock (Empress of the island)

**Impel Down** — Undersea · _explored_

A vertical underwater prison built to hold the worst criminals in the world, six levels deep.

**Problem:** Ace is scheduled for execution, and reaching him means breaking into the most secure prison in the world completely alone.
**Resolution:** Luffy fights through all six levels, and desperation forces him into uneasy alliances with people he'd otherwise call enemies.
**Outcome:** Luffy reaches his brother, but only in time to march with him straight into the war already gathering at Marineford.

What happened here:
- Luffy breaks in alone to save his brother, and breaks half the prison in the process
- An unlikely alliance of former enemies is forged behind bars

Characters tied to this island: Portgas D. Ace (imprisoned here), Jinbe (ally made here)

**Marineford** — Paradise · _explored_

Navy Headquarters, built at the very base of the Red Line — the site of the single deadliest war the pirate world had seen in decades.

**Problem:** The Navy plans a public execution of Ace specifically to draw out and destroy Whitebeard's entire crew.
**Resolution:** Whitebeard's crew, the Warlords, and Luffy's desperate rescue attempt collide in an all-out war neither side fully wins.
**Outcome:** Ace and Whitebeard both die on the same battlefield, permanently ending the era they defined and reshaping the balance of power across the seas.

What happened here:
- Portgas D. Ace is lost in front of the entire crew
- Whitebeard's death reshapes the balance of power across every sea

**Foreshadowing:** Garp's grief and Sengoku's public revelation of Luffy's parentage here plant the political weight that follows Luffy for the rest of the story.

Characters tied to this island: Edward Newgate (Whitebeard) (falls here), Sengoku (Fleet Admiral)

**Mary Geoise (Marijoia)** — Holy Land · _explored_

The World Government's capital, built atop the Red Line at the point directly opposite Reverse Mountain — home to the Celestial Dragons, the Five Elders, and whatever Imu actually is.

**Problem:** A gathering meant to unite allied kingdoms becomes the site of a framed assassination that threatens to unravel that alliance instead.
**Resolution:** The frame job against Sabo is exposed as an inside job rather than an act of the Revolutionary Army.
**Outcome:** The World Government's internal rot becomes visible even to some of its own allied kingdoms, straining trust that took decades to build.

What happened here:
- Sabo is framed for the murder of King Cobra here
- Hosts the Levely, a summit of allied kingdoms' royalty held roughly every four years

**Foreshadowing:** The empty throne room glimpsed here, and the figure called Imu occupying it, is one of the story's most tightly guarded ongoing mysteries.

Characters tied to this island: Saint Charlos (resident Celestial Dragon), Imu (hidden ruler)

### Fish-Man Island Saga

**Fish-Man Island** — Undersea · _explored_

A city under the sea, home to fish-men and merfolk still carrying the scars of a century of surface-world prejudice.

**Problem:** Decades of surface-world prejudice against fish-men have curdled into a plot by Hordy Jones to wipe out humanity in revenge.
**Resolution:** Luffy ends the plot by force but resolves the underlying grudge by talking, not just fighting.
**Outcome:** The island's long-standing wound with the surface world starts, tentatively, to heal, just as Big Mom's territorial reach reminds everyone the New World is next.

- Big Mom's territorial reach is felt even this far below the waves

Characters tied to this island: Jinbe (native son of the island)

### Dressrosa Saga

An abandoned weapons-testing island split down the middle between fire and ice, hiding an illegal human experimentation lab.

**Problem:** Caesar Clown is running illegal, fatal experiments on kidnapped children under cover of an abandoned weapons test site.
**Resolution:** The Straw Hats, Trafalgar Law, and a Navy unit team up despite deep mutual distrust to shut the lab down.

- A twisted child-experimentation scheme is shut down for good
- An uneasy alliance forms between the Straw Hats, Law, and G-5

**Dressrosa** — New World · _explored_

A sunlit kingdom of toys and gladiators, secretly run for a decade by a Warlord who never lets go of a grudge.

**Problem:** Doflamingo has secretly ruled the kingdom for a decade through a Devil Fruit that turns people into living puppets.
**Resolution:** Luffy defeats Doflamingo directly, ending both his hidden rule and his hold over the kingdom's people.

**Baltigo** — New World · _explored_

Known as the Island of White Soil — a windswept, snow-covered outpost that served as the Revolutionary Army's hidden headquarters for years before its location was finally exposed.

**Problem:** Blackbeard's crew tracks the Revolutionary Army's hidden base here specifically to seize weapons they'd just captured from Doflamingo.
**Resolution:** The Revolutionary Army evacuates before Blackbeard's crew can capture them along with the base.
**Outcome:** Baltigo is destroyed and its location burned, forcing the Revolutionaries to relocate their entire headquarters.

What happened here:
- Blackbeard's crew tracks the island down and destroys it to seize weapons the Revolutionaries had just captured from Doflamingo's trade port
- The Revolutionary Army escapes in time and relocates to Momoiro Island afterward

Characters tied to this island: Monkey D. Dragon (led the army from here)

**Kamabakka Kingdom** — New World · _explored_

A kingdom on Momoiro Island ruled by Emporio Ivankov, which became the Revolutionary Army's new headquarters once Baltigo was destroyed.

**Problem:** The Revolutionary Army needs a new headquarters after Baltigo's destruction, with no guarantee this one stays hidden any longer.
**Resolution:** Ivankov offers the kingdom he already rules as the army's new base of operations.
**Outcome:** The Revolutionary Army regains solid footing, and the kingdom quietly becomes a training ground for outsiders like Sanji as well.

What happened here:
- Becomes the Revolutionary Army's base after Baltigo's destruction
- Sanji trains here during part of the two-year timeskip

Characters tied to this island: Emporio Ivankov (ruler), Sanji (trained here)

### Four Emperors Saga

**Zou** — New World · _explored_

An island that is, quite literally, the back of a living elephant, home to a clan of minks still loyal to a country in exile.

**Problem:** Sanji has vanished to answer a summons from his birth family, and the minks of Zou are bracing for an attack tied to Wano's stolen throne.
**Resolution:** The crew reunites with their allies and finally hears the full, and much larger, truth about Wano's occupation.
**Outcome:** The story's focus widens from one crew's problems to an entire occupied country waiting for its chance to fight back.

What happened here:
- The Straw Hats reunite with Sanji's crewmates after his disappearance

Characters tied to this island: Kin'emon (reveals Wano's history)

**Whole Cake Island** — New World · _explored_

A candy-built territory ruled by one of the Four Emperors, where a wedding is used as a trap for the whole crew.

**Problem:** Sanji's own family is forcing him into a political marriage that Big Mom intends to use to wipe out the Straw Hats entirely.
**Resolution:** The crew disrupts the wedding and pulls Sanji out, at the cost of making a permanent enemy of an Emperor.
**Outcome:** Sanji rejoins the crew for good, but the Straw Hats now stand openly against one of the Four Emperors heading into Wano.

What happened here:
- Sanji is pulled back from an arranged political marriage
- Big Mom's territory becomes an open enemy of the Straw Hats

Characters tied to this island: Charlotte Linlin (Big Mom) (antagonist), Sanji (nearly married off here)

**Wano Country** — New World · _explored_

An isolated, sword-and-samurai nation sealed off from the outside world for decades under a shogun who was never who he claimed to be.

**Problem:** Kaido and a puppet shogun have ruled Wano through fear for twenty years, ever since the death of the country's rightful lord.
**Resolution:** An alliance of samurai, minks, and pirates raids Kaido's stronghold directly and ends his rule in a single decisive battle.
**Outcome:** Wano reopens to the world for the first time in generations, and Luffy's victory over an Emperor puts him on a completely different scale in the eyes of the world.

What happened here:
- Kaido's twenty-year rule over the country is ended in an all-out raid
- Luffy's bounty and reputation reach a scale the whole world takes notice of

**Foreshadowing:** The truth about the God Valley Incident, only referenced in passing during Wano, turns out to connect Kaido, Big Mom, and Whitebeard's entire generation.

Characters tied to this island: Kaido (antagonist), Kozuki Momonosuke (restored heir)

## Glossary

- **The Void Century** — A hundred-year stretch of history that the World Government has erased from every record — reading about it anywhere is treated as a crime, which is exactly what makes it worth investigating.
- **Poneglyphs** — Massive stone tablets carved in a script only a handful of scholars can read, scattered across the world and holding pieces of the buried Void Century history.
- **Road Poneglyphs** — A specific set of four Poneglyphs that, together, mark the route to the final island, Laugh Tale.
- **The Will of D.** — A recurring middle initial shared by several pivotal figures — Luffy, Ace, Teach, Rocks, and others — whose significance is treated in-story as an ominous, unexplained pattern the World Government fears.
- **The Three Ancient Weapons** — Pluton, Poseidon, and Uranus — legendary weapons of mass destruction from the Void Century era, each powerful enough to reshape the balance of the world if wielded.
- **Laugh Tale** — The final island of the Grand Line, and the resting place of the treasure One Piece. Only Gol D. Roger's crew has ever reached it.
- **One Piece** — The legendary treasure left behind by the Pirate King, whose exact nature is still unknown — its existence alone is what triggered the entire Grand Age of Pirates.
- **Devil Fruits** — Mysterious fruits that grant a single supernatural power to whoever eats them, at the permanent cost of the ability to swim.
- **Haki** — An inner spiritual energy every living thing possesses to some degree, trainable into three recognized forms: Observation (sensing others), Armament (hardening one's own body or striking incorporeal enemies), and the rare Conqueror's (imposing one's will directly onto others).
- **The Four Emperors** — The title given to the four most powerful pirate captains in the New World at any given time — a rotating seat, not a fixed group, as the raid on Wano proved.
- **The Worst Generation** — A cohort of twelve pirates, all with bounties over 100 million berries, who rose to notoriety around the same time and have been colliding with each other and the World Government ever since.
- **The Seven Warlords of the Sea** — A now-dissolved system in which the World Government pardoned powerful pirates in exchange for acting as its enforcers when needed — a compromise both sides mostly resented.
- **Buster Call** — An overwhelming Navy fleet strike authorized only by the highest levels of the World Government, intended to erase a target — island, population, and all — rather than merely defeat it.
- **Celestial Dragons (World Nobles)** — Descendants of the founding rulers of the World Government, who live above ordinary law and treat the rest of the world's population as beneath consideration.
- **The Five Elders** — The secretive ruling council that sits above the Navy and the Celestial Dragons themselves, answering only to whatever Imu actually is.
- **The Straw Hat Grand Fleet** — Seven independent crews, formed at Dressrosa, who pledged loyalty to Luffy without folding into his crew directly — they act on their own but answer his call.
- **Log Pose** — A compass that doesn't point north — instead it locks onto the magnetic field of whichever island is next on a Grand Line route, since normal compasses are useless there.
- **Conqueror's Haki** — The rarest form of Haki, letting its user overwhelm the will of weaker-willed people around them just by exerting pressure — said to appear in only one in several million people.
- **Seastone** — A rare mineral that drains a Devil Fruit user's strength just by touching them, acting like a solid, portable chunk of the sea itself. It's the Marines' standard tool for restraining one.
- **Devil Fruit Awakening** — An extremely rare stage of mastery where a user's power stops being confined to their own body and starts reshaping the world around them instead. Only a handful of the story's strongest fighters have ever reached it.
